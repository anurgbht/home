- Prediction of Overloaded Transmission Grid Elements

Enhancing operational security for Transmission Service Operators (TSOs) through the prediction of overloaded transmission grid elements involves the following key components:

  • Data Integration: We extracted relevant features from diverse data sources, including historical generation patterns, consumption data, grid topology information, and weather data. This comprehensive dataset serves as the foundation for our predictive models.
  • Model Development: Nuanced predictive models were meticulously trained using the integrated dataset. These models are designed to identify potential overloaded elements within the transmission grid.
  • Early Warning System: Our system provides early warnings to TSOs when the predictive models detect potential grid element overloads. This proactive approach allows TSOs to take mitigative actions in advance, reducing the risk of operational disruptions.
  • Operational Insights: Throughout the project, valuable insights were generated from the analysis of grid data. These insights not only enhance data quality but also contribute to a deeper understanding of the operational dynamics of the transmission grid.

- Unsupervised anomaly detection coupled with supervised classification

In the realm of IoT sensor data analysis, ensuring accurate failure tagging is paramount. Our approach combines unsupervised anomaly detection with supervised classification, utilizing the following key technologies and strategies:

  • Unsupervised Anomaly Detection: To address the frequent absence or incorrectness of failure tagging in IoT sensor data, we implemented unsupervised anomaly detectors. These detectors utilize reconstruction error-based techniques to accurately tag time series sensor data with anomalies.
  • Generation of Upstream Features: The tagged anomalies serve as valuable labels. We leveraged these labels to generate upstream features, enhancing the dataset for further analysis.
  • Supervised Classifier: The enriched dataset, containing both sensor data and tagged anomalies, was utilized to train a supervised classification model. This model is designed to categorize and predict failure events accurately.

- Machine Learning for Sticker Formation Detection

Addressing the issue of sticker (rupture) formation on the surface of slabs cast in the steel solidification process is critical to maintaining production quality. Our approach involves machine learning techniques, bolstered by the following key technologies and strategies:

  • Sticker Formation Signature: Temperature signals recorded by thermocouples embedded on the mold wall exhibit distinct signatures during sticker formation events. These signatures serve as vital indicators of the occurrence of stickers.
  • Advanced Machine Learning: We employed advanced machine learning techniques for pattern recognition in time series data, specifically focusing on the innovative feature engineering to capture and characterize sticker formation signatures effectively.
  • Real-World Deployment: The developed solution is currently deployed in Tata Steel's production process. It has significantly enhanced sticker detection accuracy, contributing to increased production quality and efficiency.

- Vessel Lineup Optimization Using Genetic Algorithm

Optimizing vessel lineups at ports is a complex task, especially considering the intricate demurrage charges associated with ship operations. Our approach harnesses Genetic Algorithm and leverages the following key technologies and strategies:

  • Demurrage Charge Complexity: Demurrage charges incurred by ships at ports involve complex non-linear rates influenced by various factors. These factors include vessel arrival times, port congestion, cargo handling, and more.
  • Genetic Algorithm-Based Optimization: We developed a Genetic Algorithm (GA) approach to address the vessel lineup optimization problem. GA is a powerful optimization technique that evolves potential solutions iteratively to arrive at an optimal or near-optimal lineup.
  • Cost Minimization: The primary objective of our approach is to arrive at a feasible vessel lineup that minimizes demurrage costs. The GA considers multiple variables and constraints to achieve this goal.
  • Feasibility Assessment: Our GA-based solution assesses the feasibility of vessel lineups, taking into account the complexities of port operations and demurrage charges. It aims to find lineups that are both cost-effective and operationally viable.

- Prediction of Engine Failure

Anticipating engine failures in advance is crucial for maintaining the reliability of machinery. Our approach leverages key technologies and techniques to predict engine failure and enable timely servicing:

  • Cox's Proportional Hazard Model: We employed Cox's Proportional Hazard model to analyze available engine-generated data. This statistical model is capable of predicting the risk of engine failure, allowing us to flag problematic engines well in advance.
  • Feature Engineering: Our approach involves engineering features based on various data sources, including vibration, pressure, and temperature data. These features are generated across different phases of engine operation, such as taxiing, takeoff, cruising, and braking.
  • Diagnosis of Preventable Ailments: By analyzing the engineered features, we can diagnose preventable ailments in the engine. This proactive approach enables us to identify issues that can be addressed through maintenance and servicing, ultimately extending the engine's operational lifespan.
  • Timely Maintenance: The primary goal is to enable timely maintenance and servicing of engines before catastrophic failures occur. By predicting engine failures in advance, we ensure the continued reliability and safety of machinery.
